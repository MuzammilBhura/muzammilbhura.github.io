About Medobo

Medobo is a medical knowledge application designed for anyone who wants to genuinely understand how medicine works — from the doctor prescribing a drug to the patient who wants to know why it was prescribed.

Our Mission: To make medical knowledge accurate, deep, and accessible — for professionals who need it at work and for everyone else who is simply curious about how the human body and medicine interact.

Who Medobo Is For

Medical Students Doctors & Clinicians Paramedical Staff Nurses & Pharmacists Teachers & Researchers Curious General Users

What Medobo Offers

Five core modules covering every dimension of medical knowledge.

🧬

MedCore

Disease articles, terminology dictionary, symptoms and signs search, lab investigations, and drug-disease links — structured as deep reference content with quick view and in-depth tabs.

💊

Pharma Vault

Comprehensive drug profiles covering mechanism of action, indications, dosing, adverse effects, interactions, pharmacokinetics, clinical trials, and therapeutic decision support.

🩺

Clinical Sense

Virtual patient simulator where you interact with a bot patient, order investigations, interpret results, and prescribe treatment. Scored on diagnostic accuracy, treatment choice, and efficiency.

🔬

Lab Investigations

In-depth profiles for blood tests, imaging, microbiology, functional tests, and procedures — covering normal values, clinical interpretation, drug effects, and more.

🤖

AI Assistant

Three AI modes — general medical concept explanation, clinical case assistance powered by Med Gemma, and a research assistant for deeper exploration of medical topics.

📖

Terminology Dictionary

A searchable library of medical terms with word breakdowns, definitions, clinical context, variants, linked diseases, and associated drugs.

Our Content Standard

Every piece of content in Medobo is built to one standard — scientifically rigorous, clinically accurate, and immediately useful. We draw from authoritative sources including FDA prescribing information, WHO documentation, major clinical guidelines, and peer-reviewed literature.
